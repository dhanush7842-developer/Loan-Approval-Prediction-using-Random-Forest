How Reliable is the Model?
Test accuracy vs. cross-validation — understanding both numbers
87% TEST ACCURACY
Think of it like a final exam. The model studied on 400 applicants, then was handed 100 it had never seen. It got 87 of those 100 correct. That's your test accuracy — performance on genuinely new data.
79% CROSS-VALIDATION
Imagine running the same exam 5 times with different students each time and averaging the scores. 79% ± 6% across all 5 rounds confirms the model is consistently reliable — not just lucky on one test.
100 DECISION TREES
A Random Forest isn't one decision — it's 100 independent trees each casting a vote. The majority wins. This "wisdom of the crowd" approach makes it far more accurate and robust than any single model.
